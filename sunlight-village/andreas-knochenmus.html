Andreas Knochenmus

Home

Birthday: 15 November 1785

Pronouns: he/him

The youngest of six children, there is a gap of more than 10 years between Andreas and Donal (b. 1774), the sibling who is closest to him in age. The birth of Andreas in 1785 came as a complete surprise to everyone in their family, especially his parents, Wilhelmina and Fergus, but everyone loved him regardless. While his elder siblings had somewhat strict childhoods, owing to the abrasive nature of their Hochlander mother, Andreas grew up completely spoiled, with both his mother and father (and pretty much all of his siblings) wrapped around his little finger.

Andreas's closest friend throughout his entire childhood was Lewis Rhyke, whose mother Ginnie was best friends with Andreas's elder sister Siobhan. The two did most everything together, though this most often included getting into trouble. The only times which Wilhelmina found it necessary to discipline Andreas were when his and Lewis's antics got someone hurt, such as when Andreas broke his arm falling out of a tree the two had been racing to climb.

After his eldest brother was arrested for murder in 1795, Andreas and his parents moved to the city, where he was introduced to master painter Peter Feigenbaum. Enamored with Feigenbaum and his artworks, Andreas quickly became his apprentice, and by the time he was 18 he was a journeyman in Feigenbaum's workshop. Dedication to painting meant that Andreas wasn't always able to accompany his parents when they visited Siobhan in Sunlight Village, and this separation began to strain his friendship with Lewis. Despite this strain, Andreas found himself developing less than platonic feelings for his friend.

Shortly after becoming a master painter in 1808, Andreas returned to visit Sunlight Village. Unbeknownst to him, Lewis's youngest brother Maxon had disappeared while in the forest with Lewis and their sister Gwen. Though Andreas is not sure exactly what he did, it's obvious that this event was the final nail in the coffin of their relationship.
